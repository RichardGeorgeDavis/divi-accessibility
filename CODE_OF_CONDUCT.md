# Code of Conduct

## Our Standards

This project is maintained as a practical accessibility-focused fork of Divi
Accessibility. Contributors, issue reporters, reviewers, and maintainers are
expected to keep discussion respectful, specific, and useful.

Examples of expected behavior:

- Use welcoming and inclusive language.
- Focus criticism on code, behavior, documentation, or accessibility impact.
- Assume good intent while still being precise about bugs and risks.
- Respect that accessibility reports may come from people directly affected by
  the issue.
- Provide reproducible details when reporting problems.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, insults, threats, or discriminatory language.
- Dismissing accessibility needs or user impact.
- Publishing private information without explicit permission.
- Repeated off-topic, promotional, or bad-faith comments.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, issues, pull
requests, or other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct.
Maintainers may also limit participation from people who repeatedly disrupt the
project.

If you need to report conduct concerns, open a private security advisory if the
concern includes sensitive information, or contact the current fork maintainer
through the contact channels listed on their GitHub profile.
